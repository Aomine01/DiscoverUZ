Unveiling the Secrets of Registan Square: A Traveler's Guide

Aziza K. Editor in Chief
calendar_today Oct 24, 2023
schedule 5 min read

Samarkand is one of the oldest cities in Central Asia and the jewel of the ancient Silk Road. Registan Square, the heart of the ancient city, is framed by three madrasahs of distinctive Islamic architecture. In this guide, we explore the history, the intricate tile work, and the best times to visit to avoid the crowds.

Walking into the square for the first time is a breathtaking experience. The sheer scale of the buildings, combined with the detailed blue and turquoise mosaics, transports you back to the 15th and 17th centuries. The Ulugbek Madrasah (left), Sher-Dor Madrasah (right), and Tilya-Kori Madrasah (center) each tell a unique story of science, astronomy, and power.

The Three Madrasahs

The oldest of the three, Ulugbek Madrasah, was built during the reign of Ulugbek from 1417-1420. It became a center for astronomical study. Look closely at the facade, and you'll see star motifs representing the sky.

Opposite stands the Sher-Dor Madrasah, constructed two centuries later. It mirrors Ulugbek's design but breaks Islamic prohibition on depicting living beings by featuring tigers chasing deer on its portal. This rebellious artistic choice makes it one of the most photographed spots in Uzbekistan.

Best Time to Visit

While the square is open all day, the magic hour happens twice: early morning at sunrise when the square is empty, and at dusk when the monuments are illuminated. Note that there is an entry fee of approximately 50,000 UZS for foreigners, and the ticket is valid for one day.

"To see Registan is to witness the ambition of Timur's empire frozen in stone and tile."

Don't forget to climb the minaret of Ulugbek Madrasah if it's open (for a small tip to the guard). The view over the turquoise domes of Samarkand is unparalleled.
